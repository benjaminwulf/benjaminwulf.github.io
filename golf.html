Swing Fundamentals

Warm-Up Stretching

  • Toe Touch
  • Side Stretch
  • Standing Twist
  • Lunges

Set-Up

  • Grips include
    • 10 finger (baseball style)
    • Overlapping
    • Interlocking
  • Aim club grooves perpendicular to target and shoulders parallel to target
  • Feet shoulder-width apart
  • Posture
    • Balance
    • Bow waist
    • Bend Knees
    • Lead sholder slightly higher
    • Eyes on ball

Chip Shot

  • Tick-tock swing like a grandfather clock
  • Sweep the grass
  • Weight on the front foot
  • Forward press at setup and impact
  • Types of chips:
    • Flop: High flight and land softly
    • Pitch: Minimal back swing and follow through
    • Bump and Run: Lower lofted club to hit and roll to target

1/2 Swing

  • Toe to sky at shoulder high
  • Tilt and turn
  • Belly button towards target at follow through
  • Hit down on ball

"L" Swing

  • Shoulder high and clubhead to the sky
  • Tuck right elbow into body
  • Keep left elbow straight
  • Accelerate knee to knee

Full Swing

  • Maintain follow through
  • Compete weight transfer
  • Keep shoulder under chin

Follow Through

  • Belly button towards target
  • Weight transfer to front foot
  • back tow towards target

Putting

  • Low and slow
  • Arms and wrist straight
  • Accelerate through ball

Understanding The Game

Tee Box

Start of each golf hole. Be safe, don't stand directly behind person hitting. Remember etiquette, and refrain from talking while others are hitting.

Course Etiquette

    Fairway

  • Once ball in play, furthest from hole hits first.
  • Replace your divots or fill with sand.

    Green

  • Quiet while others putt.
  • Person farthest from hole putts first.
  • Keep your shadow out of persons line to the hole.
  • Do not step in the line of others.

Be Aware

    Yelling FORE!

  • Do NOT hit unless safe distance from other players.
  • Yell FORE if your ball flies towars other players.

When you hear FORE, cover head and duck for saftey

Putting

  • Remove flagstick when putting on green.
  • After putting, place flag back.
  • Do not run or drag feet on green surface.

Boundary Markers and Hazards

    Yellow Hazard

    Defined by yellow stakes. Indicates lakes or standtraps. You can always play your ball from the hazard:

  • Count original stroke.
  • Add one penalty stroke.
  • Drop ball behind, no closer to hole, as far back as you want.

    Red Lateral Hazard

    Defined by red stake. Indicated rivers, creeks, and waste areas. You can play from lateral hazard. If you drop:

  • Count original stroke.
  • Add one penalty stroke.
  • Drop ball within two club lengths of where ball entered hazard, no closer to hole.

    Out Of Bounds (O.B.)

    Defined by white stakes and indicates parking lots, driving ranges, and backyard, etc. You cannot play from O.B.

  • Rehit from original spot.
  • Count original shot plus add one penalty stroke.

Grounding of club in hazard is not permitted.

  • Add one stroke penalty.
  • In Lateral hazard you have option to play or take penaltys stroke.
  • In sandtraps, do not ground club.

Penalties and Drops

    Lost Ball

    Five minutes to look for lost ball prior to taking penalty. Must re-hit from original spot.

    Example

  • Original shot
  • +1 penalty stroke
  • Replay from original location

Unplayable Lie

Choose one stroke penalty and drop of two club lengths or take call back in line with the flag.

Free Drop

  1. Cart path, sprinkler head, drain, immovable obstructions, putting green of another hole.
  2. Abnormal conditions such as casual water, ground under repair.

Golf Course Ready

Scoring

  • Stoke Play: Cumulative amounts of shots/strokes in round of play.
  • Match Play: Player with fewest strokes wins the hole. Player with most holes is winner.

Bogey and Over Par

  • Bogey: +1 over par on a hole
  • Double Bogey: +2 over par on a hole
  • Triple Bogey: 3+ over par on a hole
  • Double ParPlayer strokes double hole par (ie 6 on par 3 or 8 on par 4)

Par

The amount of strokes for a skilled golfer as designed by course architect.

  • Par 3: 0 to 250 yards
  • Par 4: 250 to 500 yards
  • Par 5: 500+ yards

Birdies and Eagles

  • Birdie: -1 under par
  • Eagle: -2 under par
  • Double Eagle (Albatross): -3 under par
